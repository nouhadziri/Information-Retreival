= The film features nearly all the show's regular cast from that time and the story of the film features various interwoven story threads that occupy the characters to varying degrees. These include the return to Number 96 of former resident Sonia  after her release from a mental asylum. Sonia is now married to newspaper journalist Duncan Hunter . Many of the residents become embroiled in the major plans for Dorrie and husband Herb's  Ruby Wedding Celebrations; those that aren't are roped in by Les to assist in his new business venture: a sauna in the building's basement. Vera endures a troubled romance with politician Nick Brent , not helped when she meets his son Tony . Meanwhile Maggie and Vera start a new business venture with Simon Carr , a character they had a previously had romantic rivalry over in the television version of Number 96 in 1972. Sonia's old friend Jack Sellars  and his new girlfriend, flight attendant Diana Moore  who has moved in to flat 6, worry about Sonia's increasingly erratic and apparently deranged behaviour. ===Release=== The film was released in May 1974 and became a major box office success.
